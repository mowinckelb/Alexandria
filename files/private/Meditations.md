## The Drafting System

Three phases, applicable to all essays:

**Phase 1: Whiteboard.** Get all info onto the whiteboard. Clarify all thoughts. Bolster. Add to. Question. Push back. Test. Add new elements. Bring up new stories, quotes, concepts. Iron out contradictions. The marginalia IS the whiteboard.

**Phase 2: Architecture.** Make options of how to weave it all together. Which tone. Which themes to emphasise. Styles. Methods. Tactics. Multiple options to choose from, not one prescribed path.

**Phase 3: Execution.** Pick one option and refine and iterate and tweak until publish.

## On Love II — Marginalia

### Literary References to Weave In

**Jane Eyre (Charlotte Brontë, 1847).** Jane's refusal to become Rochester's mistress despite intense passion — she chooses moral integrity over feeling. The architecture matters, not just the love. Rochester's journey from lust/power/control to genuine covenant love after his humbling (fire, blindness, loss of Thornfield). Jane returns to him not because of the spark — he is now blind, maimed, diminished in every way the modern model would measure — but because of who he is underneath. Published 1847, right in the hinge period. Same conversation as Middlemarch (1871-72), Emma (1815), Pride and Prejudice (1813), Vanity Fair (1847-48).

**Wittgenstein.** "Love is not a feeling. Love is put to the test. One never says of pain, 'that was not a true pain,' it lasted for so short a time." From *Culture and Value*. Love verified by endurance, not sensation. A philosopher of language saying the word "love" doesn't refer to what we think. Says in two sentences what On Love I took several paragraphs to argue. Quote directly.

**Donne — "A Valediction: Forbidding Mourning."** "Like gold to aery thinness beat" — love that stretches without breaking, becoming more luminous as it extends. The compass image: one foot fixed, the other roaming, always connected. Donne was a metaphysical poet AND Dean of St Paul's Cathedral. He IS the faithless-Christian-to-faithful-Christian journey.

**Wuthering Heights (Emily Brontë, 1847).** Brontë wrote Heathcliff as a WARNING about destructive passion. The modern audience misreads her warning as aspiration. A novelist who saw the danger, and 178 years later a culture that proves her right. → constitutional signal extracted to Love.md

**Shakespeare.** Supports BOTH sides. Passionate love poetry (eros) AND Sonnet 130 anti-courtly-love. "My mistress' eyes are nothing like the sun." The bridge between the two positions.

**Aristotle (attributed).** "The sign of an intelligent mind is the ability to entertain an idea without accepting it." The graceful play with ideas. Connects to "sit with this" framing.

**"Negotiate with entropy"** — potentially too pretentious but the concept is right. Maintaining romance over decades requires actively fighting decay. Reconsider whether the phrase earns its place.

### The Monolith / Free Solo Metaphor

Major new metaphorical framework. The setup: standing in the cold shadow between two monoliths — history (ancient covenant) and modernity (post-1960s romance-first). Modernity generates a tidal wave sweeping everyone over a cliff.

**Stage 1 — Swimming against the tide.** Exhausting. Everyone else going with the current.
**Stage 2 — The cliff.** Even escaped the current, towering stone cold cliff. The ancient model demands courage, faith, commitment. No obvious handholds.
**Stage 3 — The decision.** Soaking wet, exhausted. Jump back into the tide (easier, warmer) or free solo up the wall.

Free soloing = Alex Honnold. No safety net. Absolute concentration. Loneliest sport. You and the rock.

Overlapping images: dark knight (standing alone, misunderstood), the penguin meme (one standing still while all march off the cliff), the arena/gladiators (lonely, uncertain, crowd against you), the darkness and doubt (NOT triumphant — terrified, don't know if you're right). The pope who doubts.

### The Anne Hathaway Scene — Needs More Weight

Love & Other Drugs (2010). v1 moved too quickly to the analytical takeaway. On Love II: stay in that room. Much longer. Let the reader sit there.

The emotional beats:
- He stayed. He held her hand until the very end.

- Don't rush to the lesson. Let the silence do the work.

Also: the Up/Pixar opening — "a sequence so quiet and so devastating that entire cinemas wept without a word being spoken. A lifetime compressed into four minutes." Same emotional territory.

**Rebecca (March 2026).** James's girlfriend. 25. Healthy, normal. Suddenly fully paralysed from the neck down. This is the oncology ward line made real — not fiction, not a movie scene. A real person. A real face. The questions it forces: does the partner stay? Will my wife stay? Would I? Would I resent them? The honest answer is "I don't know" — and that honesty is the essay. The pope who doubts, applied to the deepest commitment. The essay must sit in that question, not answer it. → constitutional signal in Love.md

### The Wild Fire vs. Controlled Fire (Expanded)

Go much deeper than v1.

**Wildfire:** Getting engulfed in a forest fire. FEELS like passion. Intoxicating. But it is not passion. It is hell. Uncontrollable. Unsustainable. Always ends in damage. This is what modern "falling in love" is.

**Controlled fire / hearth fire:** May never burn with that same terrifying intensity. That is the point. You control it. You can throw gasoline on it when you want. Agency. It is controlled. Intentional. Yours.

**The primitive wonder of fire:** "If you didn't know any better that would be the reasonable thing to conclude" — early humans thinking fire was alive. Fire moves, grows, consumes, dies, can be fed, can be starved. Of course they thought it was alive. The controlled fire metaphor gains depth: you are in a relationship with something alive. You tend it. You respect it. You feed it. It keeps you alive. That is marriage.

Modern culture glamourises the wildfire and dismisses the hearth fire as "settling." The hearth fire is not settling. It is the only fire that lasts.

### The "Mature Love" Framing Rule

Benjamin believes his framework is the more mature version. But explicitly does NOT want to say this in the essays. It makes people defensive and diminishes the argument to a status claim. The essays should EMBODY maturity without CLAIMING it. Show, don't tell. The Picasso principle: you don't announce that you've been up the mountain. The view is in your eyes.
