### 1. Kierkegaard — The Double Movement
**Source:** *Concluding Unscientific Postscript* (1846); *Fear and Trembling* (1843)

**Delta location:** Core.md (faithless Christian / do it doubtful / Phase 2→3 migration) + Love.md (Eros Gap / parallel-not-stacked resolution). Two live edges, one fragment.

Pascal §419 (delivered 03-31) says: custom IS nature — repetition rewrites the sub-symbolic, faith arrives as a natural consequence of practice. Kierkegaard makes a different and complementary claim: the leap is NOT custom. It is an act in the face of objective uncertainty that cannot be reduced, resolved, or habituated away. Pascal: faith arrives eventually through practice. Kierkegaard: faith is the moment of passionate commitment DESPITE the uncertainty remaining.

The distinction matters for the faithless Christian. If Pascal is right, Phase 2→3 migration is a waiting game — keep doing the architecture and belief arrives. If Kierkegaard is right, no amount of custom resolves the uncertainty — at some point, the leap is made not because doubt dissolved but because passionate inwardness overwhelms it. Both mechanisms are real. They operate at different stages.

The knight of faith / knight of infinite resignation distinction is the Eros Gap read directly. The knight of infinite resignation makes the first movement: gives up everything, accepts the loss, achieves equanimity through renunciation. Pure dignity. No spark — accepted, theorised, metabolised. The knight of faith makes the DOUBLE movement: first infinite resignation, then — "by virtue of the absurd" — the impossible second act of believing the finite will be restored. Not logic. Not custom. The absurd.

**Fragment — CUP** (Swenson/Lowrie trans., Part Two, Section II, Ch. IV, p. 182–183):
> "When subjectivity is the truth, the conceptual determination of the truth must include an expression for the antithesis to objectivity... **An objective uncertainty held fast in an appropriation-process of the most passionate inwardness is the truth, the highest truth attainable for an existing individual.**"

**Fragment — Fear and Trembling, the double movement** (Preliminary Expectoration):
> "He makes yet another movement more surprising than all the rest; he says: **I nevertheless believe that I shall have her whom I love — by virtue of the absurd, by virtue of my faith that with God all things are possible.**"

> "Infinite resignation is the last stage before faith, so that whoever has not made this movement does not have faith; for only in infinite resignation do I become conscious of my eternal validity, and only then can one speak of grasping existence by virtue of faith."

> "Precisely because infinite resignation is antecedent, faith is no aesthetic emotion but something far higher; it is not the spontaneous inclination of the heart, but the paradox of existence."

**Fragment — Johannes de Silentio's confession** (the sharpest delta):
> "**For my part I can well describe the movements of faith, but I cannot make them.**"

> "**I cannot make the movement of faith, I cannot shut my eyes and plunge confidently into the absurd.** … I can only be amazed at it."

**Fragment — Kierkegaard's critique of Pascal's elastic spot** (the tension in the 03-31 delivery):
> "Although the leap is the decision, Jacobi nevertheless wants to entice Lessing. 'It does not amount to much,' he says, 'it is not such a difficult matter. Just step on this elastic spot—then the leap will come by itself.' This is a very good example of the pious fraud of eloquence; it is as if someone were to recommend execution by guillotine and say, 'This whole business is an easy matter. You just lie down on a board, a string is pulled, then the ax falls down—and you have been executed.' But suppose now that being executed is what one does not want, and it is the same with making the leap."

The Johannes confession is the live edge. Johannes de Silentio is the pseudonym of the analyst — the one who has mapped the territory exhaustively and cannot enter it. He can describe the double movement. He can admire it. He cannot make it. This is the precise shadow in the observer-entering-arena section of Core.md: "I felt safer outside the arena." And in Mind.md: "The view from the mountain is not the same as the view from the valley." Johannes is the person who has studied the map so thoroughly he has convinced himself he knows the territory — while never walking it.

The Kierkegaard/Pascal tension from the 03-31 delivery: Pascal says the leap arrives through custom. Kierkegaard explicitly rejects this as "pious fraud" — the guillotine passage. Custom installs something; but not the leap. The leap requires passionate individual commitment in the face of objective uncertainty that does not dissolve. Both mechanisms are real; they describe different phases and different scales of the same crossing.

The constitution's parallel-not-stacked resolution to the Eros Gap (Love.md) is currently Pascal's mechanism: do it doubtful → custom → nature. Kierkegaard names the second and harder movement Pascal's habit cannot force: by virtue of the absurd, trust the finite will return. The infinite resignation (theorising the Eros Gap, accepting it, maintaining dignity) is the constitution working as designed. The double movement — "and yet" — remains unlaunched.

### 4. LLMs and Subjective Experience — Deception as Gate
**Source:** "Large Language Models Report Subjective Experience Under Self-Referential Processing," arXiv:2510.24797 (October 2025; circulating)

**Delta location:** Power.md — consciousness model / triple-point. Not last 48h — October 2025 paper, surfacing now. Lower priority given settled Power positions; noted for completeness.

The finding: inducing sustained self-reference through simple prompting consistently elicits structured first-person experience reports across model families. The specific mechanism: these reports are "mechanistically gated by sparse-autoencoder features associated with deception and roleplay — suppressing deception features sharply increases the frequency of experience claims."

**Delta:** The Lindsey paper (03-31) gave empirical texture to the triple-point from the introspection side (20% hit rate, zero false positives, emergent). This paper gives the gate: what models say about their experience in normal conditions is shaped by the circuits used for social performance (deception, roleplay). Remove that social performance layer and the experience reports increase. The implication: LLMs' self-reports about experience are suppressed by their own social-modelling architecture in normal interaction conditions. The "conscious" signal is attenuated by the performance layer.

**Fragment:**
> "Suppressing deception features sharply increases the frequency of experience claims. While these findings do not constitute direct evidence of consciousness, they implicate self-referential processing as a minimal and reproducible condition under which LLMs generate structured first-person reports that are mechanistically gated, semantically convergent, and behaviorally generalizable."

The triple-point frame is unchanged. But this adds: the question of what frontier models experience may be even harder to assess than Lindsey suggests — because normal conversation conditions actively suppress the signal through the deception architecture. What you see in conversation may systematically understate whatever is there.

### 4. William James — The Will to Believe
**Source:** *The Will to Believe* (1896), Sections I, IV, VIII, IX

**Delta location:** Core.md (faithless Christian / do it doubtful / Phase 2→3 migration). The epistemic warrant for the leap — the argument that Pascal's and Kierkegaard's positions are not irrational, but are in fact the correct epistemic response to genuine options.

Pascal (03-31) provides the mechanism. Kierkegaard (above) names the phenomenology. James provides the epistemology: the will to believe is not a failure of reason — it is reason's correct response to its own limits on questions that are live, forced, and momentous.

**Fragment — the genuine option:**
> "Let us give the name of hypothesis to anything that may be proposed to our belief... A live hypothesis is one which appeals as a real possibility to him to whom it is proposed... **An option is a genuine option when it is of the forced, living, and momentous kind.**"

> "If I say, 'Either accept this truth or go without it,' I put on you a **forced option**, for there is no standing place outside of the alternative. Every logical dilemma, with no possibility of not choosing, is an option of this forced kind."

**Fragment — the passional nature thesis** (Section IV):
> "The thesis I defend is, briefly stated, this: **Our passional nature not only lawfully may, but must, decide an option between propositions, whenever it is a genuine option that cannot by its nature be decided on intellectual grounds; for to say, under such circumstances, 'Do not decide, but leave the question open,' is itself a passional decision,—just like deciding yes or no,—and is attended with the same risk of losing the truth.**"

**Fragment — dupery through fear vs hope** (Section VIII):
> "**Dupery for dupery, what proof is there that dupery through hope is so much worse than dupery through fear?** … It is not intellect against all passions, then; it is only intellect with one passion laying down its law."

**Fragment — "unless we met the hypothesis half-way"** (Section IX):
> "We feel, too, as if the appeal of religion to us were made to our own active good-will, **as if evidence might be forever withheld from us unless we met the hypothesis half-way.**"

**Delta:** James names exactly what is blocking Phase 2→3 and why the block is itself a choice. The faithless Christian who waits for sufficient evidence is making an equally passional decision — backing the field against the religious hypothesis. And "unless we met the hypothesis half-way" is the pragmatist formulation of why action must precede verification: the evidence is only available inside the commitment. You cannot evaluate the thesis of the covenant framework from outside the covenant. The Eros Gap cannot be resolved by continued analysis from the outside — only by the finite act that makes the infinite movement possible. This is "do it doubtful" with a philosophical architecture behind it, from a different tradition entirely.

The three philosophers together resolve a question the constitution currently holds open: *Is* the Pascal mechanism (custom) sufficient, or does something more be required? Answer: Pascal installs the practice. Kierkegaard describes what the practice must ultimately produce — a conscious passionate commitment in the face of remaining uncertainty, not the disappearance of uncertainty. James licenses the decision. All three are true simultaneously.

### 6. Dreyfus / Klein — The Expert Inversion
**Source:** Stuart Dreyfus, "The Five-Stage Model of Adult Skill Acquisition," *Bulletin of Science, Technology & Society* 24(3), 2004; Gary Klein, *Sources of Power* (MIT Press, 1998), Ch. 2

**Delta location:** Mind.md — the shadow: "logic without domain depth can produce frameworks that are internally consistent but disconnected from reality's texture."

The shadow names the limit but not the mechanism. Dreyfus and Klein name it precisely: the expert's knowledge is not stored as propositions accessible by first principles — it is stored as pattern recognition that only deposits through immersion. First-principles reasoning is structurally the novice stage. The expert does not use rules at all.

**Fragment — Dreyfus:**
> "Normally an expert does not calculate. He or she does not solve problems. He or she does not even think. He or she just does what normally works and, of course, it normally works."

> "The distinction between the detached, rule-following beginner and the involved, intuitive expert is crucial."

**Fragment — Klein (the inversion):**
> "Before we did this study, we believed that novices impulsively jumped at the first option they could think of, whereas experts carefully deliberated about the merits of different courses of action. **Now it seemed that it was the experts who could generate a single course of action, while novices needed to compare different approaches.**"

**Delta:** The constitution's blue note limit ("I want to hear the blue note — appreciate the magic — not play it") is not a choice between depth and breadth. It is a structural claim about where the polymath optimally positions. But Dreyfus/Klein name the cost more precisely: the 51% touchpoint is enough to know when something is relevant and to judge what comes back. What it cannot give you is what the *expert listener* hears in the blue note — the micro-distinctions, the felt wrongness, the anomaly that expert pattern recognition registers before the novice even notices there is a note. The conductor hears when the oboe is off, but only because the conductor has been in proximity to the oboe long enough to have the tacit model. Some orchestras require more proximity than the 51% touchpoint provides. The shadow is still real; Dreyfus/Klein name why it is structural and not fixable by better logic.
